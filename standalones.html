Cat & Mice

Cat & Mice is the first(ish) in a series of pieces I've written over the years in collaboration with a bounty of highly profficient instrumentalists. The collaboration has been facilitated through the HKU (the college I attend) and each time I've been highly pleased with the results. April 2024 will mark the finale of this series, with what promises to be an explosive finale with the Zuilens Brass Orchestra.

Writing for solo cello was a daunting task. My first fear was the lack of options: what are you supposed to do with not just a single instrument, but a monophonic composition!?

As it turns out, there's lots you can do to make a piece such as this special. My challenge quickly transformed from a fear over lack of options, to an almost greedy desire to explore the bounds of what I could get a performer to achieve. The result is a long, varied piece of which I am still as proud as I could be of a single composition.

My only regret is that I couldn't get myself to tone down the skill level just a smidge, as the cellist had a very short time to practice. Still, I think he did an amazing job and I do quite enjoy 'hearing' him sweat throughout the performance.

Beasts Awakening

Beast Awakening was originally a school project— A technical challenge centering the concept of "modulation". This particular assignment ended up being a personal favorite composition of mine, however. I love escalation as well as musical phrases that feel truly L A R G E and imposing. That combined with the fact that this piece is where I stopped worrying and learned to love clarinet.

This was a very intuitive piece to write for me. It helped to have a clear assignment, but I also chose and arrangement and musical structure that fit the intensity with which I like to imbue my music. It took a couple of weeks to complete, as the assignment was comprised of multiple sub-assignments, which sort of forced me to pay attention to details I may have overlooked had I been invited to rush it for a quick grade.

Flórianne's Canon

Flórianne's Canon is a song I wrote for my sister. She's a year younger than me and though she never got into music the way I did, she used to play the first two bars of this piece all the time sitting next to me behind the keyboard. When I started composing more seriously, she asked me to finish it, and I gladly obliged!

There was no pressure on this piece, which really helped me find my flow in a genre that's a little bit out of my ballpark. I tend to write my classical music with a very contemporary mindset, but this was a fairly straight-forward classical canon. I just wanted to make sure those original two bars really shaped the rest of the song, and I think that worked out great!
